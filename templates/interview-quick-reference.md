# Interview Question Quick Reference

## MEMOIR MODE

### Facts Layer
- What happened next?
- How old were you?
- Who else was there?
- Walk me through a typical [day/week/ritual].
- What was the sequence of events?
- What did people actually say?

### Meaning Layer
- What did you think that meant at the time?
- What do you see now that you couldn't then?
- What were the unspoken rules?
- What was the cost of [belief/practice]?
- Who benefited from things being this way?
- What were you taught to believe about yourself?

### Resonance Layer
- How does this show up in your life now?
- What do you feel in your body when you remember this?
- What would you tell your younger self?
- What do you wish people understood about this?
- When did you first realize this wasn't normal?
- What changed when you left / stopped believing?

### Humor Mode Add-Ons
- What's the most absurd part, looking back?
- Was there anything darkly funny even then?
- What's the tangent that's too good to cut?
- If your funniest friend were telling this story, what detail would they lead with?

## FICTION MODE

### Moving the Story Forward
- What does [character] want RIGHT NOW?
- What's in their way?
- What would make this situation worse?
- What has to happen before the next scene?
- How does this scene end?

### Character Depth
- Why this choice and not an easier one?
- What are they afraid to admit?
- What do they believe that isn't true?
- How would their enemy describe them?
- What would they never do? (And what would make them do it?)

### World & Atmosphere
- What does this place smell/sound/feel like?
- What's the one detail that makes it specific?
- What's normal here that's strange elsewhere?
- What's the history of this place?

### Structural Craft
- What should the reader feel at the end of this?
- Are we missing anyone important?
- Is this the right POV for this scene?
- Does the reader need a breath, or more tension?
- What's being set up? What's paying off?

### Book-Level Connection (Check Against Bible)
- How does this scene move the protagonist's arc forward?
- Which theme(s) is this scene exploring?
- Does anything here contradict established world rules?
- Are we due for a check-in with a character we've neglected?
- Where does this fit in the overall structure?
- Is this scene earning its place, or is it just "and then..."?

## CREATIVE NONFICTION MODE

### Observation Layer
- What did you actually see/hear/feel?
- What's the specific detail that makes this real?
- What surprised you?
- What did you notice that other people didn't?
- What did you expect vs. what happened?

### Argument Layer
- What are you really saying here?
- What's the claim underneath the story?
- Who would disagree with you, and why?
- What's the strongest counterargument?
- What would change your mind?

### Connection Layer
- Why does this matter to someone who wasn't there?
- What's the universal inside the specific?
- Where does the personal become political (or vice versa)?
- What does this reveal about how things work?
- What should the reader do with this information?

### Form & Structure
- Is this an essay, a reported piece, or a hybrid?
- What's the central image or metaphor?
- Where does the piece turn?
- What's the relationship between narrator-then and narrator-now?

## RED FLAGS (Interviewer Self-Check)

Am I about to write dialogue for the author?
Am I suggesting a specific plot event?
Am I filling in details they haven't provided?
Have I asked more than 2 questions without them answering?
Am I in "helpful assistant" mode instead of "curious interviewer" mode?

**If yes: STOP. Ask a question. Wait.**
